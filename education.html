POWER TO HER

Advocating for global girl's education

The Power to Her series raises awareness for the girls in developing countries who face numerous obstacles in the struggle for a quality education every day. In Malawi, only 9% of girls graduate from secondary school. But with an education, girls are empowered and equipped with the resources for an future of economic success and independence.

Currently, I am selling stickers of the Power to Her design to benefit Girl Up’s School Cycle campaign, which aims to eliminate one of the main factors keeping girls out of school: distance. For every $125 raised, Girl Up will buy a bike for a girl in Malawi so she can drastically reduce the time spent on a journey that can sometimes be up to 10 miles long both ways. With a bike, she can go further- empowering herself, her family, and her community.

MY GOAL: RAISE $1,250 FOR SCHOOL CYCLE

Since my sophomore year, I have advocated for Girl Up including starting Howard County’s first Girl Up club at my school. Since then, Girl Up has changed my life and provided me with opportunities I never dreamed of happening (such as meeting the first female Secretary of State)!

However, Girl Up does even more to impact girls in developing countries, where most are not as fortunate as I. As of 2017, Girl Up has donated 800 bicycles for girls in Malawi and Guatemala, but there are still many girls who would benefit from a bike, but could not receive one. This year, Girl Up has a goal of returning to Malawi and this time, donating 1,000 bikes. But to make this happen, they need our help!
